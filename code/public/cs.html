Predicting steel-plates defects

I joined a Kaggle playground series competition for the month of March 2024, with the task of predicting steel-plate defects. The playground series is organized by Kaggle, is open to everyone of any level with Kaggle merchandise to win. It mainly serves the purpose of training for other competitions and improving one's knowledge in the machine learning domain.

The task of the competition is as follows: to predict the various defects on steel plates using the synthetic dataset provided and any model of your choice. Submissions are evaluated using area under the ROC curve for each of the defect categories and then averaged to give a final score.

I achieved a score of 0.86375, which ranked me 1443 out of 2199. The top score of the competition achieved 0.89782.

Tech Stack: Python, Pyspark, Machine learning, Jupyter Notebook

Project tag: Machine learning

Prediction, Image processing & Transfer learning

This assessment was separated into 2 parts: Song genre prediction and Image processing & transfer learning.
Part 1 focused on predicting the genre of a song using one input feature, and then two through deep neural network models and methods. The dataset is the multilingual lyrics one from Kaggle comprising of two files: train and test. The train dataset will be used for training, validating and initial testing, and the test dataset for final testing.
Part 2 utilized the CIFAR-100 dataset, where we were tasked to randomly split the dataset into 2 subsets of 50 classes each and performa range of image processing and transfer learning operations.

A score of 71% was awarded for this project.

Tech Stack: Python, Deep learning, Keras, Tensorflow, Machine learning, Jupyter Notebook

Project tag: Machine learning
