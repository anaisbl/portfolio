Why art and computer science?

Art has always held a special place in my heart—I've been drawing since before I could even write! I've used it to capture my dreams, fears, ideas, and everything I see or imagine. While I chose not to pursue formal education in art, I kept it as a personal passion. In my free time I continue to hone my skills and take on creative projects with plenty of freedom and exciting ideas.

Art and computer science may seem worlds apart, and in some ways, they are. I enjoy how different they are in both content and approach. Art fuels my creativity, while computer science challenges the analytical side of my brain. Both let me get "in the zone," whether I'm training a classification model or picking a color palette for a new project. Growing up, I had minimal interest in computers. I enjoyed learning tips, tricks, and shortcuts, but the science behind computers felt so far out of my reach.

That changed during the pandemic, when I took an elective coding class in the final year of my bachelor's degree. It opened my eyes to the world of computing, and I thought, "So, this is what it's all about!"
Five years later, I've earned a master's in computer science, understand OOP, and can deploy my own websites and data science projects. Time has been a great teacher, and I'm proud of how far I've come. I can't wait to look back in 2030 and see how much further I've gone!
