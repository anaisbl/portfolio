About

Why art and computer science?

Art has a special place in my heart. I started drawing before I even learned to write! Drawing my day dreams, my nighmares, my goals, my many trains of thoughts, anything and everything I saw, heard about and imagined.
I didn't want to mix business and pleasure so much, which is why I didn't pursue a higher education in the subject. However, in my free time I hone my skills and take on certain projects with a lot of creative freedom and super cools ideas!

Art and computer science might have seemed miles apart decades ago, and some might consider them so still to this day, including me. I like how different they both are in terms of content and approaches. Art stimulates my creative brain, and computer science stimulates the "scienc-y" side of it. I love how both allow me to fall into the zone, wether I'm trying to figure out how to deploy through Github actions or choosing a color palette for a new project. My interest in computers was minimal from a young age, I was agile with technology, I liked learning the little tricks, tips and shortcuts but I always thought the world of computer science was out of reach for me.

The pandemic changed that for me, I was introduced to web development thanks to an elective class I took in my last year of bachelors, and I thought "Wow, this is what coding is!". 5 years later, here I am, graduated masters in computer science, knowing what OOP means and being able to deploy my own website and data science projects! Time is a beautiful thing, I am proud of where I stand today and I can't wait to look back in 2030
